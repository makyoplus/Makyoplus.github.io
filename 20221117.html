20221117: Sairento Cityscape Appreciation

Sairento has been a real fixture for me in my time spent in VR. Pretty much whenever I fire up SteamVR (after landing in my custom steamVR environment) I'll play a few rounds of Sairento to warm up and adjust to the construct, before diving into whatever other game I'm going to play.
I've always been struck by the cityscapes - whilst being, on the one hand, fairly generic iterations of the **future-Tokyo** ubiquitous to the Cyberpunk genre ("the megacity of the Tokyo Cluster, 2066 AD" apparently), on the other hand I think they've nailed it with the quirky skyscrapers, rainy atmospheres, little shopfronts, signage and graffiti etc...
The maps have a vibe that for me brings up memories of sitting in front of the PS1, squinting at low poly cityscapes outside the playable area and wondering what's out there...


















... and here's a copy of Dogue thrown down in the street by a resident of NeoTokyo. Amazing what the future holds in store for us isn't it?
